With such a great engine at our disposal, you may have wondered how I created it?

It starts a whole year (or more) ago, where I simply wanted to create an engine from scratch. ~\newline
 It was called {\bfseries{Round Engine}} and it was pretty feature heavy, but eventually the project got canned.

The issue was with the same issues as most other engines have. The project was too complicated... ~\newline
 and it didn\textquotesingle{}t perform as well as I would have hoped. Round Engine was unfortunately bogged down by -\/ ~\newline
 compatibility, features no one could use, and bad code. As such I privated the repo.

Cut to\+: Early 2023, where I was thinking about optimization and was rather frustrated since no other engine -\/ ~\newline
 quite fit what I needed. Each one had it\textquotesingle{}s quirks that made it unsuitable for something I could build mods on. ~\newline
 So I created assorion. An engine where no expenses were spared. It\textquotesingle{}s not very compatible with the base game at all, ~\newline
 and things are handled far differently than the base game\textquotesingle{}s however that was certainly for the better.

Assorion was originally called {\bfseries{MKG Engine}}, but once my friend ({\itshape Byzol} / {\itshape Barzil}) proposed the name "{}\+Assorion"{}, ~\newline
 we just had to roll with the name.\hypertarget{md_documents_2History_autotoc_md10}{}\doxysection{\texorpdfstring{ⓘ \texorpdfstring{$\vert$}{|} What Does Assorion Do differently?}{ⓘ | What Does Assorion Do differently?}}\label{md_documents_2History_autotoc_md10}
PS\+: This will be expanded on in it\textquotesingle{}s own MD file.

We\textquotesingle{}re gonna skip code changes and focus more on the {\itshape philosophy} of Assorion.

\begin{quote}
WARNING! ~\newline
 Highly opinionated stuff ahead. ~\newline
 Also rather mean stuff as-\/well, sorry to any one offended by this. \end{quote}
The reason why we call Assorion "{}\+The Linux of FNF engines"{}, is because of the way I like to handle it. ~\newline
 Assorion is not meant to be the most feature-\/rich engine, it\textquotesingle{}s not even really suposed to be the easiest to work on. ~\newline
 The goal of Assorion is to have an engine, that is tiny at it\textquotesingle{}s core, a tiny engine where features can be added on later.

The issue I have with most engines is how they often include things that I consider "{}bloat"{} -\/ ~\newline
 (i.\+e\+: Discord\+RPC, Cutscenes, Lua, Options that people don\textquotesingle{}t need), and I didn\textquotesingle{}t want something like that. ~\newline
 I wanted an engine where those things could be added, but aren\textquotesingle{}t in the base version.

Because...\hypertarget{md_documents_2History_autotoc_md11}{}\doxysubsection{\texorpdfstring{I don\textquotesingle{}t want to water down any mods built on this engine, just because some people might use a feature!}{I don't want to water down any mods built on this engine, just because some people might use a feature!}}\label{md_documents_2History_autotoc_md11}
That\textquotesingle{}s basically how I\textquotesingle{}d rather do things.

Almost everything in Assorion is meant to be things that every engine should have, and features that other -\/ ~\newline
 people can use, it\textquotesingle{}s not meant to be about having {\bfseries{ALL}} of the features.

But as for the code side of things?

I try to keep it as simple as humanly possible. I try to re-\/use functions when I can. ~\newline
 I try to use return, break, continue where-\/ever I can. ~\newline
 And I try to consider the low-\/level cost of the features I implement.

It\textquotesingle{}s not about sheer performance, it\textquotesingle{}s about code consistency. Assorion should be easier to work with because of this.

\begin{quote}
NOTE! ~\newline
 Although Assorion\textquotesingle{}s logic is essentially perfected, it might not perform {\itshape {\bfseries{THAT GREAT}}}... ~\newline
 Sadly, the engine is still bogged down by Flixel\textquotesingle{}s rendering which is slow and ineffecient. ~\newline
 You will probably only notice a big difference on integrated graphics (or something), or -\/ ~\newline
 on really bad CPUs. ~\newline


Just letting you know. \end{quote}
Lastly, Assorion is basically opinion driven. ~\newline
 You don\textquotesingle{}t have to agree with me or use this engine, so don\textquotesingle{}t feel too upset if you don\textquotesingle{}t care enough about this. ~\newline
 I just want an engine that works for me, and is small at it\textquotesingle{}s core. If you don\textquotesingle{}t care, or if you want to just have -\/ ~\newline
 a feature rich engine, then Psych Engine isn\textquotesingle{}t a bad choice.
